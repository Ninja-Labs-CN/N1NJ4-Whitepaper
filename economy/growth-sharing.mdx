## Growth Sharing

The most important economic commitment City Zero makes is this:

> **Everyone who helps build the city gets a share of what the city becomes.**

This is not a vague promise. It is the architectural purpose of the contribution tracking and treasury systems. Every verifiable contribution to City Zero is recorded on Injective, and the growth sharing mechanism uses that record to determine how value is distributed as the city grows.

## Why Growth Sharing?

Traditional platforms and protocols extract value from builders and distribute it upward. Developers who created critical infrastructure for early Ethereum, for example, received no structural share of Ethereum's success unless they happened to hold ETH or received grants.

City Zero is designed differently. The people who build the infrastructure, create the applications, write the documentation, run the community calls, and deploy the AI agents that make the city function are not service providers. They are co-builders. They should share in what they help create.

<Info>
  Growth sharing is not a loyalty program. It is the city's mechanism for recognizing that builders are stakeholders, and that the city's success is inseparable from the contributions of those who built it.
</Info>

## How Growth Is Measured

City Zero tracks growth across multiple dimensions:

| Metric | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Active Citizens** | Number of unique N1NJ4 identities actively participating in the ecosystem |
| **On-Chain Activity** | Transaction volume, governance participation, and application usage |
| **Developer Output** | Verified grant deliverables, deployed contracts, and shipped applications |
| **AI Agent Activity** | AI resident services rendered and interactions completed |
| **Treasury Growth** | Increase in the City Zero treasury value over time |
| **Ecosystem Expansion** | New projects and brands launched within the City Zero ecosystem |

## Contribution Tiers

Not all contributions are equal, and the growth sharing system reflects this:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tier 1: Infrastructure Builders">
    Developers who build core City Zero infrastructure, including identity systems, governance contracts, and treasury mechanisms, receive the highest growth sharing weight. These contributions form the foundation everything else depends on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tier 2: Ecosystem Builders">
    Developers who build applications, tools, and services within City Zero that serve the broader citizen population. Grant-funded projects, incubated brands, and community tooling fall in this tier.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tier 3: Community Contributors">
    Citizens who contribute through governance participation, community organization, content creation, and other non-code activities that grow the city's culture and population.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tier 4: AI Contributors">
    AI agents whose activity demonstrably adds value to the City Zero ecosystem, through services provided, automation enabled, or insights generated, participate in growth sharing proportional to their verifiable contribution.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Long-Term Alignment

The growth sharing mechanism creates a direct, on-chain link between a citizen's contribution history and their economic stake in the city's future. This achieves something traditional equity models cannot: transparent, verifiable, community-governed distribution of value to everyone who helped create it.

As City Zero grows, the growth sharing pool grows with it. Early builders who were here when the city was being constructed have the deepest contribution records and therefore the greatest stake in what the city becomes.

The city remembers. The chain never forgets.
