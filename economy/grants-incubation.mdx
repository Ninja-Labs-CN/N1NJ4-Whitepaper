## Grants & Incubation

Ninja Labs maintains two programs designed to fund and support developers who want to build in City Zero: the **Community Grant Program** and the **Incubation Program**. Together, these form the primary economic onramp for new builders entering the ecosystem.

## Community Grant Program

The Community Grant Program provides direct funding for projects that benefit City Zero and its citizens. Grants are community-governed, approved by N1NJ4 citizens through on-chain voting rather than a central committee. They are milestone-based, with funds released in tranches tied to verifiable deliverables. All grant activity is recorded on Injective, creating a transparent public record. And they are non-dilutive: grants do not require equity or token allocation to Ninja Labs.

## Incubation Program

The Incubation Program goes beyond one-time grants. It is a structured support pathway for developers who want to build a lasting brand or project within City Zero.

### What Incubation Provides

| Support Type | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Sustained Funding** | Multi-phase funding tied to project milestones, not a one-time grant |
| **Technical Support** | Direct access to Ninja Labs engineers for architecture review and technical guidance |
| **Community Distribution** | Introduction to the N1NJ4 community as an endorsed City Zero project |
| **Co-building** | Active collaboration with Ninja Labs and other city builders where appropriate |
| **Mentorship Network** | Connection to experienced builders in the Injective and broader web3 ecosystem |
| **On-Chain Credentialing** | Your project's activity and deliverables are recorded on-chain as part of City Zero's contribution history |

### What Incubation Does Not Require

Ninja Labs does not take equity in incubated projects. There is no exclusivity requirement. You can collaborate with other ecosystems while in the incubation program. There is also no mandatory token distribution to Ninja Labs or the City Zero treasury.

<Note>
  The philosophy is simple: we want you to succeed independently. A City Zero that graduates successful, independent projects is more valuable than one that retains permanent dependencies.
</Note>

## Applying

Grant and incubation applications are submitted through City Zero's governance portal and include a project description, team background, milestone plan with concrete deliverables and success metrics, funding request, and an explanation of how your project will contribute to City Zero's growth metrics.

Applications are reviewed by the community during a defined discussion period before moving to a governance vote. All applications and their outcomes are publicly visible on-chain.
