## Why Injective?

City Zero is not blockchain-agnostic. The choice to build on **Injective** is deliberate and architectural. Injective provides a unique combination of properties that make it the right foundation for a digital city.

---

## Injective at a Glance

Injective is a high-performance Layer 1 blockchain purpose-built for finance and decentralized applications. Key properties relevant to City Zero:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="EVM Compatible" icon="ethereum">
    Injective supports Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM) compatibility, meaning any Solidity smart contract can run on Injective. Developers building in City Zero can use familiar tools and languages.
  </Card>
  <Card title="High Throughput" icon="bolt">
    Injective processes thousands of transactions per second with near-instant finality. City Zero's on-chain activity — identity operations, governance votes, economic transactions — can happen at scale without congestion.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Low Fees" icon="dollar-sign">
    Transaction fees on Injective are a fraction of those on Ethereum mainnet. This makes micro-transactions viable — critical for a city where everyday activity, not just large financial operations, happens on-chain.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Native DeFi Primitives" icon="chart-line">
    Injective has built-in support for order books, derivatives, and other financial primitives at the protocol level. City Zero's economic layer can leverage these natively without additional infrastructure.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Why Not Other Chains?

City Zero evaluated several blockchain foundations before selecting Injective:

**Ethereum Mainnet** — Prohibitively expensive for high-frequency city activity. Gas costs would make everyday on-chain participation inaccessible.

**Polygon / L2s** — While lower cost, these chains lack Injective's native financial primitives and the tight integration with the broader Cosmos/IBC ecosystem that enables cross-chain city expansion.

**Solana** — Developer tooling differs significantly from EVM. The City Zero developer community is predominantly EVM-native. Migration friction outweighs performance benefits.

**Injective** — EVM compatibility, sub-second finality, near-zero fees, native DeFi primitives, and an active ecosystem builder community. The right choice for a city that will handle both financial and social on-chain activity at scale.
