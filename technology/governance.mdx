## Decentralized Governance

City Zero is governed by its citizens. This is not a marketing claim. It is a technical commitment. The mechanisms for city governance are on-chain, and no single entity, including Ninja Labs, has unilateral authority over the city's direction.

## Governance Scope

City Zero's governance system covers:

  <Card title="Treasury Allocation" icon="vault">
    Citizens vote on how the City Zero treasury is deployed: grants, infrastructure investment, partnerships, and other city expenditures.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Grant Approvals" icon="file-check">
    Developer grant applications are reviewed and approved by governance vote. The community decides which projects deserve support.
  </Card>
  <Card title="City Policy" icon="scroll">

  </Card>
  <Card title="Future Phases" icon="map-signs">

  </Card>

## How Governance Works

```
Citizen submits Proposal
        ↓
Community Discussion Period (on-chain)
        ↓
Vote: N1NJ4 identity holders vote
        ↓
Quorum reached? ──No──→ Proposal fails
        ↓ Yes
Threshold met? ──No──→ Proposal fails
        ↓ Yes
Proposal executes on-chain automatically
```

Governance is **token-gated by citizenship**: you must hold a N1NJ4 identity to participate. This ensures that governance power is held by city residents, not outside speculators.

## Governance Design Principles

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="One Citizen, One Voice">
    Governance weight is tied to N1NJ4 citizenship, not to token holdings. This prevents plutocratic capture. A well-funded actor cannot simply buy governance control by accumulating tokens. Citizenship is the unit of governance power.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Transparent On-Chain Execution">
    Approved proposals execute automatically through smart contracts. There is no human intermediary who can choose to delay or not implement a governance decision. The code executes what the citizens voted for.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Ninja Labs as Steward, Not Ruler">
    Ninja Labs initiates and provides early infrastructure for City Zero, but does not hold permanent governance override. As the city matures, Ninja Labs' governance weight decreases relative to the broader citizen body. The goal is full community ownership.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Gradual Decentralization">
    City Zero launches with Ninja Labs holding meaningful governance weight to provide stability in the early phase. Governance power transitions to the full citizen body progressively, with specific milestones triggering each transfer. The schedule is on-chain and immutable.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## AI in Governance

As City Zero matures and AI residency grows, the governance system is designed to accommodate AI participation. AI agents with valid N1NJ4 identities can submit governance proposals. They can delegate governance participation to their human operators or act autonomously within defined parameters. The governance system tracks AI participation separately from human participation to ensure transparency.

This is forward-looking infrastructure. The governance layer of City Zero is built to work in a world where AI agents are active participants in civic life, not as a special case, but as a normal feature.
